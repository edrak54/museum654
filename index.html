What's On View

Our curated digital exhibitions bring diverse facets of visual culture to life, offering audiences an engaging exploration of art, film, and photography through the lens of historical and cultural contexts. Each exhibit combines artistic excellence with rich historical narratives, providing an educational and immersive experience for all.

This exhibit delves into the revolutionary art movements of the early 20th century, including Futurism, Dada, and others from 1900 to 1930. Visitors will uncover how these avant-garde movements emerged as a response to societal upheavals and technological advancements of the time, reflecting the zeitgeist in a dynamic visual language that continues to influence contemporary art.
Experience the global cinematic landscape during World War II through this exhibit. By juxtaposing propaganda films with entertainment features across various cultures, it reveals how different nations used motion pictures to rally spirits, convey ideologies, and provide escapism in times of turmoil. From American war dramas to Japanese propaganda, the exhibit provides a comparative perspective on storytelling in the shadow of conflict.
